BayesianBahn predicts when your train will actually arrive — as a full
probability distribution, not a single number.

Pick a station, pick a train from the live departure board, and see:

* the median predicted arrival time,
* an 80% credible interval,
* the full delay distribution as a chart,
* the probability of arriving within 5 minutes of schedule,
* the historical cancellation rate.

Predictions are empirical: they come from months of real historical runs of
that exact train at that station (collected from Deutsche Bahn's public IRIS
API, CC BY 4.0), reweighted for recency, weekday, and the train's current
live delay. Trains without history get an honest Bayesian prior estimate.

The app talks only to the keyless public IRIS timetable endpoint, needs no
account, no API key, no Google services, and collects no data.
